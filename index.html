PUSH BACK THE DARKNESS
Darkness cannot drive out darkness;
Only light can do that.
Hate cannot drive out hate;
Only love can do that.
-- Martin Luther King, Jr.
On January 29th 2017, a dark event in Canadian history occurred when a mosque in Quebec City was targeted by a terrorist attack. That day, the darkness of ignorance and hate cost the lives of 6 people, injured 19 others, and left 17 children without their fathers.
The goal of the Push Back The Darkness initiative is to push back the darkness of ignorance and hate with the light of knowledge and friendship. This will involve several things:
Statements in the House of Commons and Senate
On January 29th, Members of Parliament and Senators will be making statements on Parliament Hill in support of this initiative.

Collecting and sharing stories and drawings
Stories and drawings are being collected from people across Canada, and these stories will be shared with our Members of Parliament and Senators, and the Canadian public via this website.

Events in communities across Canada

Across the country, we are asking people to place a light in their windows on January 29th at 8pm local time. This will be a symbol of your commitment to push back the darkness of ignorance and hate in your community
